🔁 Sequencing Rules
Red Light → Heat → Cold (Full Stack)

Red light primes mitochondria. Heat loads cardiovascular system. Cold closes the loop. HRV improvement is maximal with this sequence. ~45 min total.

Evening: Sauna → Cold → Bed

Core temp drops post-sauna. Cold accelerates the drop. Melatonin onset advances significantly. Your strongest sleep intervention. 3+ nights/week shifts WHOOP scores within 2 weeks.

Cold Before Strength Training

Morning cold shower on waking is fine — the gap before training is enough. Don't do cold plunge immediately pre-session. Cold after strength = fine.

Post-Late BJJ (after 9pm)

Skip sauna — raises core temp and pushes sleep onset back. Cold shower only post-BJJ. Save sauna for the next day.

SLEEP PROTOCOL

Optimised for high-stress CEO · 4 kids · travel variability

🌙 Why Sleep Is Priority #1

Poor sleep elevates cortisol → raises fasting glucose (your 102 reading is directly affected) → drives visceral fat storage → impairs cognitive performance. You cannot outwork bad sleep. Chronic restriction undoes every other intervention in this plan.

Evening Wind-Down Stack

9:00pm — Screens Off, Work Finished

Blue light suppresses melatonin for 2–3 hours. Phone across the room. Non-negotiable.

9:00–9:10pm — Red Light Panel (10–15 min)

Dim room. Sit close to panel. Directly signals melatonin onset. Use this before sauna on evenings when you do both.

9:10–9:35pm — Sauna (65–75°C, 20–25 min)

Lower temp on sleep nights. The core temperature drop after the sauna is the mechanism. Don't go maximum heat — you want recovery heat, not performance heat.

9:35pm — Cold Shower (2 min)

Completes the core temp drop. Closes the sleep cycle. Don't skip this after sauna.

9:35–10:00pm — Wind-Down

  • Dim all lights — no overhead
  • Magnesium glycinate 400mg
  • Room temp 18–20°C
  • Conversation, reading, or silence only

10:00–10:15pm — Lights Out Target

You're naturally tired by 9pm — use it rather than overriding it with screens. 7–7.5 hrs in bed minimum.

Morning Anchor

On Waking — Natural Light First, Cold Shower Within 10 Min

Outside or open curtains immediately. Morning light sets circadian clock and anchors cortisol awakening response — directly improving sleep pressure the following night.

NUTRITION RULES

No permanent calorie counting · 3 rules · 3-week calibration window · IF 16:8

01
Lunch is your controllable meal
When eating out for work: protein + vegetables, no bread, no fries, no sugary drinks. Grilled fish, salad with protein, sashimi, chicken + greens — every restaurant has a version. One better choice per lunch, every day, is the single highest-leverage nutritional habit you can build right now.
02
Cut fast food from twice to once per week
That single swap removes 1,000+ excess calories per week. Don't eliminate it — just halve it. One family takeout night is fine. Perfection is the enemy of consistency here.
03
Protein anchor at every dinner
Jen's cooking is already good — don't change it. Ensure dinner always includes substantial protein: fish, chicken, red meat 2–3×/week. Target 140–160g protein daily. At 48, muscle preservation while losing fat depends entirely on this. Protein is the one macronutrient you cannot afford to undercut.
